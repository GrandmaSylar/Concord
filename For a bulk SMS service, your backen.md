For a bulk SMS service, your backend architecture needs to be asynchronous to handle thousands of messages without timing out. Build your backend around three core routes: Upload/Validation, Campaign Dispatch, and Delivery Reports (Webhooks).1. The Core RoutesRoute 1: Contact Upload & ValidationMethod: POST /api/v1/sms/campaigns/uploadPurpose: Accepts CSV/JSON files, parses phone numbers, normalizes formats (e.g., to E.164 standard), and validates against local/global Do-Not-Call (DNC) registries.Payload: Multipart form-data with the file or an array of numbers and the message body.Route 2: Trigger/Queue Bulk DispatchMethod: POST /api/v1/sms/campaigns/sendPurpose: Immediately queues the parsed list. Never send bulk SMS synchronously in a web request. Instead, this route pushes individual message jobs to a message broker (e.g., Redis with BullMQ) and returns a 202 Accepted status with a campaign ID.Payload: {"campaign_id": "...", "message": "...", "sender_id": "..."}Route 3: Delivery Report (DLR) WebhookMethod: POST /api/v1/sms/webhooks/statusPurpose: An endpoint exposed for your SMS gateway (e.g., Twilio or Arkesel) to send real-time message delivery updates. Your backend consumes this to update message statuses (Delivered, Failed, Undelivered) in your database.2. Recommended SMS Gateway PartnersTo connect your backend to telecom networks globally (or locally), integrate with these established SMS APIs:Twilio SMS API: The industry standard for developer flexibility and reliable, global carrier routing.Infobip SMS API: Excellent choice for enterprise-grade security, global reach, and omnichannel routing.Arkesel API: A highly localized, reliable, and cost-effective SMS provider specifically optimized for African telecommunications and businesses.3. Backend Architecture ChecklistAsynchronous Processing: Utilize background workers (like Celery for Python or BullMQ for Node.js) to process your send queue.Direct vs. Grey Routes: Ensure your provider provisions Direct Routes (connecting directly to Mobile Network Operators) rather than Grey Routes. This ensures your Sender ID isn't blocked and guarantees delivery.Rate Limiting: Implement rate limiting on your API to prevent spam or malicious account usage.Sender ID Registration: Make sure your backend can handle dynamic Sender IDs, as regions like Ghana and the wider African market often require pre-registration of business names to avoid messages being flagged as spam.
